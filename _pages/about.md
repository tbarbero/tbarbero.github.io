I'm a senior at the Scripps Institution of Oceanograpy @ UC San Diego. I will be getting my B.S. in Oceanic and Atmospheric Sciences with a minor in Mathematics Decemeber 2020. I hope to enter a graduate program starting Fall 2021. <br />
My primary interests are in large-scale atmospheric dynamics particularily waves, general circulation, teleconnections, and influences of these regional weather and extreme events. Some others interests lie in clouds and mountain meteorology. <br /> 
My current research is investigating a diurnal cycle of background dustiness in the Salton basin which we think is forced by thermally-driven mountain-valley flows and convection due to the deepening of the atmospheric boundary layer. We conduct research through a combation of observational analysis and modeling (Advisor: Amato Evan). <br />
During the 2021 Summer, I will be working at Princeton/GFDL as a CIMES intern on Evaluating the Impacts of Initial Conditions on Hurricane Movement in High-Resolution Global Models under Jan-Huey Chen. <br />
I'm also passionate about teaching and mentoring. The atmosphere infinitely beautiful and fascinating, and I hope to inspire the same motivation in students through teaching and mentoring, especially those who are underrepresented in the physical sciences.
In my leisure time, I love to surf, catch up on TV-shows and admire clouds.
