I graduated from the [University of California, San Diego](https://ucsd.edu/) and [Scripps Institution of Oceanography](https://scripps.ucsd.edu/) with a B.S. in Oceanic and Atmospheric Sciences and Minor in Mathematics.\
My research interests are broadly in atmospheric and climate dynamics, climate variability and teleconnections. For example, I hope to look at the role of atmospheric waves on climate and weather. Furthermore, I hope to understand the effect of ENSO/MJO on tropical cyclones (TCs). 
In Summer 2021, I will be working as a [CIMES](https://cimes.princeton.edu/news/welcome-cimes-2021-summer-interns) intern at Princeton and GFDL, investigating hurricane track biases between the SHiELD (GFDL), IFS and GFS models under the guidance of Jan-Huey Chen.\
I'm also passionate about teaching and mentoring. The atmosphere infinitely beautiful and fascinating, and I hope to inspire the same motivation in students through teaching and mentoring, especially those who are underrepresented in the physical sciences.\
Typically, you'll find me surfing, binge watching TV-shows, or just casually admiring clouds.
