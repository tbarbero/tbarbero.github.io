I graduated from the [University of California, San Diego](https://ucsd.edu/) and [Scripps Institution of Oceanography](https://scripps.ucsd.edu/) with a B.S. in Oceanic and Atmospheric Sciences and Minor in Mathematics.\
My research interests lie in Atmospheric Dynamics on all spatial scales. Specifically, I want to better understand how variabiltiy in global circulation influences changes in regional weather and its associated weather and hydrological extremes (e.g., heatwaves, extreme precipitation). I also want to better understand the dynamics of extratropical and tropical cyclones and their effects on other atmosphere-ocean processes.\
During the 2021 Summer, I will be working as a [CIMES](https://cimes.princeton.edu/) intern at Princeton and GFDL on Evaluating the Impacts of Initial Conditions on Hurricane Movement in High-Resolution Global Models under Jan-Huey Chen.\
I'm also passionate about teaching and mentoring. The atmosphere infinitely beautiful and fascinating, and I hope to inspire the same motivation in students through teaching and mentoring, especially those who are underrepresented in the physical sciences.\
In my leisure time, I love to surf, catch up on TV-shows and admire clouds.
